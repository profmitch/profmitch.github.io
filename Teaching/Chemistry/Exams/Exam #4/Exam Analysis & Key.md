## ✅ Analysis of True/False Questions (Q26–Q35)

### **Q26**

*A catalyst lowers the activation energy (Ea) of a reaction but does not change ΔH.*  

- **Correct:** True  
- **Notes:** ΔH depends only on reactants/products, not on the pathway. Catalyst only lowers Ea.

---

### **Q27**  

*If the temperature of an endothermic reaction at equilibrium is raised, Le Chatelier's Principle predicts the equilibrium will shift to favor the reverse reaction (exothermic direction).*  

- **Correct:** False  
- **Notes:** Raising temperature favors the endothermic (forward) direction, not reverse.

---

### **Q28**  

*Alpha particles are the most penetrating type of common radiation and require thick concrete or lead shielding.*  

- **Correct:** False  
- **Notes:** Alpha particles are least penetrating (stopped by paper/skin). Gamma rays are most penetrating.

---

### **Q29**  

*A strong acid differs from a weak acid primarily because the strong acid is present at a higher concentration.*  

- **Correct:** False  
- **Notes:** Strength refers to degree of ionization/dissociation, not concentration.

---

### **Q30**  

*Balancing a chemical reaction equation is done by changing the subscripts on the atoms of one or more of the compounds in the reaction equation.*  

- **Correct:** False  
- **Notes:** Balancing is done by adjusting coefficients, never subscripts.

---

### **Q31**  

*The half-life of a radioisotope is the time required for half of the sample to decay.*  

- **Correct:** True  
- **Notes:** This is the definition of half-life.

---

### **Q32**  

*In performing a chemical reaction, the theoretical yield is the maximum amount of product that can be formed from the initial amounts of reactants, while the actual yield is what you actually got from doing the reaction.*  

- **Correct:** True  
- **Notes:** Standard definitions of theoretical vs. actual yield.

---

### **Q33**  

*Collision theory in part states that for a chemical reaction to occur, particles must collide with correct orientation and sufficient energy.*  

- **Correct:** True  
- **Notes:** This is exactly the principle of collision theory.

---

### **Q34**  

*Water (H₂O) can act as both an acid and a base depending on the reaction partner.*  

- **Correct:** True  
- **Notes:** Amphoteric nature of water.

---

### **Q35**  

*If the pH of a solution is 6.00, the corresponding pOH of the solution must be 10.00 because pH + pOH = 16.00.*  

- **Correct:** False (intentional error)  
- **Notes:** At 25 °C, pH + pOH = 14.00. So if pH = 6.00, pOH = 8.00. The statement is deliberately incorrect.
