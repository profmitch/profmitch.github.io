=========== Gemini start ============
That is a fantastic distinction to bring to this conversation. Since you are in a seminar about the pedagogy of AI, you’ve hit on one of the most important debates in the field: **AI as a "Calculator" (Executive)** versus **AI as a "Tutor" (Instrumental).**

To answer your question directly: **Yes, I can absolutely operate in both modes.** However, because most AI models (including myself) are default-optimized for "Helpfulness" and "Efficiency," our natural state is often **Executive Help-Seeking.** We are designed to solve the problem you put in front of us as quickly as possible.

Here is how I function in both modes and how you can switch me between them.

---

### 1. Executive Help-Seeking (Default)

In this mode, I am a "task-completer." If you give me a math problem, I solve it. If you ask for an essay, I write it.

* **The User Goal:** "I want this finished."
* **My Output:** A direct answer, a completed product, or a step-by-step solution that requires no further work from you.
* **The Risk:** As your seminar speaker likely mentioned, this leads to "knowledge bypass," where the task is done but no learning occurs.

### 2. Instrumental Help-Seeking (Socratic/Tutor)

In this mode, I act as a "scaffold." I deliberately withhold the final answer to guide you toward discovering it yourself.

* **The User Goal:** "I want to understand this."
* **My Output:** Hints, clarifying questions, analogies, or identification of the "next logical step" without doing the math for you.
* **The Power:** This promotes "productive struggle," which is essential for long-term retention.

---

### How to "Switch" Me

Since I don't know your intent unless you tell me, you have to use **Prompt Engineering** to force me into Instrumental mode. You can do this by setting a "Persona" or "Constraint."

**Try asking me a question using one of these "Instrumental" frames:**

* **The Socratic Prompt:** *"I'm working on [Problem X]. Instead of giving me the answer, act as a Socratic tutor. Ask me a question that helps me find the next step myself."*
* **The "Hint Only" Prompt:** *"I am stuck on this concept. Give me a hint that points to the underlying principle, but don't show me the solution yet."*
* **The "Identify the Error" Prompt:** *"Here is my work so far [Insert Work]. Don't tell me the right answer; just tell me which line has the logic error so I can try to fix it."*

---

### A Live Example

If you gave me the equation :

* **Executive Mode:** "Subtract 5, then divide by 3. ."
* **Instrumental Mode:** "To get  by itself, we first need to move the constant. Looking at the , what is the opposite operation you could perform on both sides to clear it?"
