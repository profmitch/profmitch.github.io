Note: if there is a loss of points in your report, it is for any of the following reasons
1) Failing to follow directions in preparing the report, such as not showing calculations. I am being lenient in scoring in deductions, whereas others would take a 5% deduction for each failed showing of calculations
2) Not recording instrument measurements/readings properly as instructed many times, such as not recording every digit on the balance for a mass
3) Not calculating decimal places for addition/substraction operations and significant digits for multiplication/division operations properly. This has been stressed since the first day of class.
4) Writing your own data--your measurements--in the report that is confusing and makes it hard to know which number belongs to which and which is used and not used.
5) Inattention to detail that indicates or suggests you did not take the assignment at all seriously or importantly that could have been a better report with minimal effort
This does not say that the report had all of these flaws, but it may have had one or more of them.

Your report will never have a loss of points for an experiment that was interrupted because of some problem beyond your control, or for a particular yield of a product or even for a mistake in reading a mass on a balance, as long as you think about the reasons for why expectations were not met for something.
